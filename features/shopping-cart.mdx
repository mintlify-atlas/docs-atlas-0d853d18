---
title: "Shopping cart"
description: "How customers add items, manage quantities, and review totals before checking out."
---

The shopping cart lets customers collect products before purchasing. Items persist across page loads so customers can shop freely and return to their cart at any time.

## Cart persistence

The cart is tied to the user's Django session. Session data is stored server-side, so cart contents survive page refreshes and browser navigation within the same session. Authenticated users benefit from extended session lifetimes configured in `SESSION_COOKIE_SECURE = True` in production.

## Cart workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Browse and add items">
    From any product listing or detail page, click **Add to cart**. The item is added to the session-backed cart immediately, with a quantity of 1 by default.
  </Step>
  <Step title="View the cart">
    Open the cart page to see all added items, their unit prices, selected quantities, and the running subtotal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Update quantities">
    Use the quantity input on the cart page to increase or decrease the number of units for any line item. The subtotal updates automatically.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove items">
    Click **Remove** next to any line item to delete it from the cart. The cart total recalculates instantly.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Proceed to checkout">
    When the cart contents look correct, click **Checkout** to move to the shipping and payment flow.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Cart totals

The cart page displays a live breakdown of costs:

| Line | Calculation |
|---|---|
| Item subtotal | `unit_price × quantity` for each line item |
| Cart total | Sum of all item subtotals |
| Shipping | Calculated at checkout based on delivery address |
| Grand total | Cart total + shipping (shown at checkout) |

All prices are displayed in Colombian pesos (COP).

## Stock validation

When a customer adds an item or adjusts a quantity, the cart checks the product's `stock_quantity` field. Requests that exceed available stock are rejected with an error message, preventing overselling.
